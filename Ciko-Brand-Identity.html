Target Audience
A1
The Stuck Builder
Ages 20–35. Has an idea but thinks they need coding skills, money, or a mentor to start. Follows Ciko because he proves none of that is required — just a willingness to start at 4AM.
A2
The Immigrant Grinder
Ages 22–38. Moved to a new country (or wants to). Navigating language, culture, isolation. Finds in Ciko's story the proof that starting from zero in a foreign place is not only survivable — it's powerful.
A3
The No-Budget Athlete
Ages 18–32. Wants to build a physique but can't afford a coach, gym, or program. Ciko's no-coach calisthenics journey gives them permission and a roadmap: YouTube, a park, consistency.
A4
The Post-Failure Rebuilder
Ages 25–45. Failed at something significant — a business, a venture, a bet. Lost money or time. Follows Ciko because his $40,000 loss is not a punchline — it's the setup to the story they're living.
Voice & Tone
🎙️
Raw
No filters. No polishing. Real numbers — $40,000 lost, 250€ to start, 200 users. Ciko says the uncomfortable things other creators skip. The audience can feel when something is true.
🎬
Cinematic
Every post should feel like a scene from a documentary. Slow. Deliberate. Intentional. Dark B-roll. One line of text at a time. Long pauses that carry more weight than a thousand words.
🔁
Relentless
The message is always forward. Never victim energy. Never asking for sympathy. The story is always: I fell. I got up. I'm still going. Every post ends with motion — not resolution, because it's not over yet.
Do's & Don'ts

✓ Do

  • Use dark backgrounds — black is your canvas
  • Write one idea per slide / per line of text overlay
  • Use real, specific numbers ($40K, 250€, 3 weeks, 200 users)
  • Show the process — not just the result
  • Let silence and negative space carry weight
  • Reference both worlds: Indonesia and Germany
  • End every piece of content with forward motion

✗ Don't

  • Use motivational speaker energy or hype language
  • Use bright colors, gradients, or loud visuals in content
  • Talk about success you haven't achieved yet
  • Spend more than 20 minutes editing a reel
  • Use stock footage or staged moments
  • Claim authority — show evidence instead
  • Use voice cloning — authenticity is the entire brand

The Biggest Differentiator — The Story Is Still Happening

Every other creator shares what they've already achieved. Ciko's entire brand is built on the fact that he hasn't made it yet — and is showing every step in real time. When SmartKartoffel gets its first paying customer, that's a reel. When he leaves the chef job, that will be the most-watched thing he ever posts. The audience isn't following a success story. They're in the middle of one.
