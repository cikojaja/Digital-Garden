# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

IT hiring managers, technical recruiters, and senior engineers evaluating candidates. They land here from a job application or LinkedIn. Context: quick first impression, 30–60 seconds, deciding whether to keep reading. Secondary: indie builders / maker community who discover Ciko through social.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio that converts skeptical recruiters into believers. The story — chef by day, self-funded AI builder by night in Hamburg — is the unfair advantage. The site must make that story credible and specific, not just quirky. Success = recruiter books a call or sends an email.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Relentless. Surprisingly warm.

## Anti-references

- Generic developer portfolios (skills grid, GitHub streak, same layout as everyone else)
- Neon/cyberpunk overload (matrix rain, excessive glow, hackercore aesthetic that reads cheap or try-hard)
- Corporate SaaS landing pages (feature grids, "trusted by 10k teams" copy)

## Design Principles

1. **The story IS the credential.** Chef + builder is rare. Make the duality visible and specific, not just mentioned in copy.
2. **Earn every pixel of darkness.** Dark theme is justified by the "4AM builder" identity, not aesthetics. Restraint makes the light elements hit harder.
3. **Show the work, not the tool.** Outcomes and real pipelines > tech badges and framework logos.
4. **Recruit-first, impress second.** A hiring manager needs to find skills, contact, and evidence fast. Don't bury them in animation.
5. **Discipline reads through restraint.** A chef who runs a precise kitchen doesn't use ten garnishes. Apply the same principle to UI.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Respect prefers-reduced-motion (animations are heavy; fallbacks needed).
